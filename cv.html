About Me

I'm a self-taught developer living and working in San Diego, California. For most of my life I had no idea what I wanted to do. Actually I thought I would make movies, but all I ever ended up doing was skating all the time and ignoring school. I lucked into a job with good pay and benefits when I was 19 (March 2000). The company was extremely large and had more than enough places that I could bounce around to so as not to get bored. In 2007 I found what I thought would be my career, until I decided to challenge myself to learn HTML and CSS.

After that there was no doubt that I wanted to be a programmer.

I spent the next couple of years learning one thing, convicing myself that was all I was smart enough to do and then surprising myself by learning something harder. I taught myself HTML and CSS then PHP, C# and then I really dove into JavaScript. I found its flexibility, simple data structures and first-class functions to be very appealing.

In late 2013, I decided to jump into the deepend and learn Haskell. I was looking for something new that would put me as far out of my comfort zone as possible. Haskell did just the trick. I'm completely addicted to the ideas of functional programming.

I'm passionate about learning, and I'm always looking for a new challenge.
